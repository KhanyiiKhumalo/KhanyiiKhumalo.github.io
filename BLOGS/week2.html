    Reflection on the readings.
    The readings this week were a lot to take in and digest but they were overall very insightful. After reading both assigned readings, I concluded that websites must be useful, approachable, and visually appealing. Websites are not only used to display information but are also used to inspire and motivate those visiting the website. Performance is also very essential, but it is closely related to the usefulness of the website. The website can only operate well if it works well and with a specific intention. The main point from Lawson, Bruce’s “Why You Should Choose HTML5” and Lawson, Bruce. “How A Screen Reader User Accesses The Web: A Smashing Video.” • Don't overuse h1> tags. Make h1 the primary heading on your website, then use h2 through h4 in the correct order without missing any steps. • When combined with aria label, section> can be used to indicate to screen reader users the start and conclusion of a specific section of a document. • For screen reader users, the tags "main," "header," "footer," and "nav" are very helpful. For people who don't use assistive technology, they are completely obvious. Use them, then. • Article can refer to any self-contained object; it's not just for blog posts.     Reflection on internet histories.
    The way we interact, conduct business, and obtain information has changed as a result of the internet. Its influence cannot be exaggerated; it has changed the way we live. The internet has made it possible for us to interact with one another in ways that were previously unthinkable thanks to its constantly developing technology. It has a past of invention, taking chances, and inventiveness, with businesses like Apple, Google, and Amazon taking the lead in creating new forms of commerce and communication.Cybercrime and security lapses are becoming more prevalent on the internet, and this has not been without its share of dark times. The internet is a strong instrument that can be used for good or bad, so it's crucial to consider the possible effects of our online behavior.Because social media is used to both promote variety and propagate hatred, the internet is a reflection of our society and culture. As we reflect on the evolution of the internet, we must work to ensure that we handle all of our online dealings with care and regard for the rights of others. We all have a part in this duty, which will only grow in importance as the internet develops.
