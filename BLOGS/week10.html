    Write a reflection on one of the readings.
    Birhane and Guest - 2020 - Towards decolonising computational sciences.pdf
    The paper makes the case that Western epistemologies and methodologies have dominated computational science, leading to a constrained and exclusive approach that does not adequately reflect the range of experiences and viewpoints throughout the world. The authors offer a paradigm for decolonizing computational science that includes acknowledging the limits of Western epistemologies, connecting with local knowledge and practices, and encouraging diversity and inclusion in the field. The paper underlines the necessity of increased cultural sensitivity and inclusion in research and stresses the value of involving many viewpoints and knowledge systems. Because it questions preconceptions and prejudices and emphasizes the value of a variety of sources, I found this essay to be thought-provoking as a language model.

    Reference:
    Birhane, A. & Guest, O., 2020. Towards decolonising computational sciences. arXiv preprint arXiv:2009.14258.
