    Reflection on the reading and understanding of UI/UX
    A work on the subject of context's importance in literary interpretation is Standard and Context, by Mailloux, published in 1983. It investigates how reading a work in a particular setting can have an impact on conventions, or the typical methods of understanding a text. According to Mailloux, context is essential to understanding a document. Conventions are not fixed; rather, they can be interpreted in different ways depending on the reader's situation. Additionally, he talks about the connections between social and literary norms, emphasizing the value of knowing a text's social setting in order to fully comprehend its meaning. As learning Web developers we can learn a range of principles about designing user interfaces that work from Mailloux (1983). Mailloux concentrates on the use of conventions in user interfaces to establish a feeling of context that can aid users in comprehending the goal and functionality of a website or application. He places emphasis on how conventions can be used to improve the usability of a website or application and can help users make choices and direct their behavior.

    Thoughts of course material
    Usually, I think it's helpful to look at the same piece from different angles. It sometimes makes sense to read the related supplementary material, in my opinion, if there are ideas in the technical lessons that I do not comprehend. I feel like I'm revising my work for the week by watching the previous classes. Since the material is being given from a variety of perspectives, I am also learning more about it. It's advantageous, in my opinion, that we have access to classes that present various viewpoints on the subject. My comprehension of the task is greatly enhanced by watching them. Your vantage point is widened, and you are given the opportunity to consider ideas or issues from various angles.
